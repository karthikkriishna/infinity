Some things repeat for a reason

Culture & Traditions

A club is mostly its repeated habits. Here's what Infinity keeps coming back to, year after year, tenure after tenure.

Recurring Traditions

Annual

Continuum / Flagship Week

The club's annual flagship fest. Renamed after a different mathematician most years since 2024 — Fourier's Fête, then Cantor's Continuum.

Since 2022

Help Sessions

Free sessions for students without a prior math background, run alongside regular coursework.

Since 2023

Math Jams

Informal, recurring sessions to chew on crowdsourced puzzles. No prerequisites, ever.

Since 2023, with Phi@I

Integration Bee

Single-elimination calculus competition. Now in its third-plus year running.

Since 2023

EMLI5

"Explain [Math] Like I'm 5" — strip the jargon from an abstract topic and explain it simply. A freshers' favorite.

2024 onward

Escape the Room

Infinity's most-repeated single event — run several times a year, not just during fest week.

Collaborators

Most of Infinity's biggest events are joint efforts. Clubs we've run things with, on record:

  • Phi@I
  • Turing
  • Movie Club
  • IMQC
  • BDF Club
  • Literary Debating Society
  • CMIT, IISER Thiruvananthapuram
